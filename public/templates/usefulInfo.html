Calling Mountain Rescue

Dial 999 or 112, ask for Police and then Mountain Rescue

Now be ready to give a CHALET report:
Casualties – number, names (and, if possible, age); type of injuries, for example, lower leg, head injury, collapse, drowning etc.
Hazards to the rescuers – for example, strong winds, avalanche, rock fall.
Access – the name of mountain area and description of the terrain. It may be appropriate to describe the approach and any distinguishing features such as an orange survival bag. Information on the weather conditions at the incident site is useful, particularly if you are in cloud or mist.
Location of the incident – a grid reference and a description is ideal.
Equipment at the scene – for example, torches, other mobile phones, group shelters, medical personnel.
Type of incident – mountain, aircraft, train, etc. Be prepared to give a brief description of the time and apparent cause of the incident.

Register for 999 text service

If you run into the trouble when on the hills you may not have enough signal to call for help, but a text message may be able to get through!

To register, text 'register' to 999. You will get a reply and will then need to follow the instructions you are sent
